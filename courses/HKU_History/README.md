## Why this folder exists

The Capstone is methodologically demanding — it needs **trans-Pacific history, military-industrial critique, digital methods, civilian voice, and non-Western frameworks** to stand up to ethics review. The courses below are the HKU undergraduate offering that supply that support.

The Capstone already passes on its primary network analysis. These courses are not required for the thesis itself — they are the **historical-training backbone** that makes the writing defensible and the comparative claims honest.

## How to use

1. **Read the main list:** [`HKU_History_Courses.md`](./HKU_History_Courses.md) for full table with relevance notes and tier markers.
2. **Prioritise Tier 1** for direct capstone use; Tier 2 for comparative support; Tier 3 for methodological foundation.
3. **Match courses to capstone sections:**
   - §2 (Literature) → Tier 1 historical surveys + HIST4015
   - §4.6 / §4.6.5 (Military-industrial critique) → HIST2154, HIST2091, HIST2142
   - §4.6.5 (Profit-driven inertia) → HIST2154, HIST2215
   - §5 (Discussion — civilian voice, non-Western) → HIST2070, HIST2161, HIST2158, HIST2219, JAPN2089
   - Methods → HIST4037 (AI + history), HIST2031 (history through film), HIST4015 (theory)
